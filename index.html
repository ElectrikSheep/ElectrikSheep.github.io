About

Few years ago I studied abroad thanks to the ERASMUS programs. Since then I like to discover new countries from a residential point of view.
After I graduated from my French Masters degree in Computer Science, I had the opportunity to work in Canada.
At first as an intern, but since I knew a few things the company decided I could be useful full time. I went from the shy intern who couldn't articulate a word to a full feldge developer being a member of a team, and oh boy, does that feel good.
The biggest change though, was my discovery of the Game Engine Unity.

At first its interface scared the beejezus out of me, but gradually, with the help of my teammates, I slowly tamed the beast. I now love it, it allows me to give life to my ideas so easily it's almost scary. How many times did I cursed my slow fingers that couldn't follow my train of thoughts fast enough.

As a developer, I find that time never behave the way it should. Most of the time I will look at the clock, write some code for what I think is 5 minutes and sadly 2 hours have gone by. I love those day where I am so focused, so drowning in work, that time flies by then I come home and dream of codes. DAMN I like that !
On the other hand … there are the other days. The boring ones, once you are done refactoring your code, everything is commented, you are waiting on client approval, it could come any seconds so you don’t want to start a new research subject … and you have nothing to do. Time stretches indefinitely, you try to stay busy but the dullness sets in your skull. I hate those days …
