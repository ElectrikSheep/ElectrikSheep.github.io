About

I'll try to make this short and avoid boring you to death
Not so long ago I was a baby developer, doing a master in Computer Science with a cool specialty in Computer Vision. Basically reading research paper, re-writing the most basic OpenGl function, ... university stuff. The best part was the computer vision applied to medical imaging, that's when I learned the human body is a wonderful piece of work, and the smallest thing can fuck you up real good. LESS BACON, MORE VEGETABLES is what I am trying to say.

Then I graduated and the first thing I learned was : "You know nothing"
One company was foolish enough to give me a chance and hired me as a Unity developer. Knowing that I had no previous C# or unity experience it was kind of a risk. After a month of being quite useless, I took on good size project and quickly rose in the ranks to become a full fledged developer.

Fast forward a year, leading on projects and stuff, basically the big boys club. I got the chances to go to Unite 2013 in Vancouver. And it was A-MA-ZING. So many gifted people, so much stuff to do with the engines. I realised I severely under-used it and spent more and more time trying stuff on my own.

Fast forward a bit more, the Augmented Reality bubble kinda burst and the projects I am working on start all looking the same to me with a different content. The most fun I had in the last month was when I worked on my own games, playing and trying new stuff.

And here I am now, trying to jump branches and leave the AR behind me to go and try my skills against Game Design. We'll see how THAT goes. By the way if you are an employer and reading this, I have the best coffee to code ratio, and I drink a lot of coffee. I also have a rather good alcohol to idea ratio but I keep that for after office hours.
